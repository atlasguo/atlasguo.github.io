Does Climate Domain
Chinese Dynasties?

Geog 572 Lab 1 - Visual Storytelling

Click Here to View Full Image

In telling the story of Chinese ancient dynasties’ rise and fall, I introduced the perspective of climate, and explore it by firstly looking at the population, and then use the time of administrative units (points) to visualized it.

One of the biggest challenges is actually the data itself. It is not too hard to draw the lines of temperature and population over time (I reference several documents and manually digitalized them), but it is really complicated to combine them with the begin and end year of places, because the years could be any time, while I only have limited “known” year of temperature and population. I wrote several python scripts to deal with this issue, trying to connect the discrete data to a continuous range by creating functions, so that given a specific year, I can provide the estimated temperature and population. It might not be objective enough, but enough for this practice.

I added roads, main roads, railways, local parks, hospitals, schools, rivers, county boundaries and so on, and labeled a lot of streets, highways as well as the main destination along with these highways. Marauder's-Map-Themed
Web Map

Geog 572 Lab 3 - Aesthetic Styling

Click Here to View the Web Map

This map illustrates the basic geographic information as well as some theme sites using the style from Harry Potter’s Marauder’s Map, such as the vintage-like brown color and fonts. The themed data is the sites related to the topic of Harry Potter located in the United Kingdom.

1) Form: The form would be normal in general, as I didn’t change the linework quite a lot. The boundaries, roads, rivers are still showing the reality. Given enough time, I do want to use the text-style line and footprint line which are from the original Marauder’s Map, as I listed in inspiration board. It will look more antique and more mysterious.

2) Color: The color has highly concentrated on light yellow (khaki/light brown) and dark brown, which looks like vintage maps in parchment.

3) Type: The type would be the most important part here. Base on the original Marauder’s Map, it is a combination of medieval Gothic, copper-plate cursive, handwritten Italic, old-style typewriter Roman in all capital letters (sometimes first letter larger), Irish/Celtic/Scottish hand writing calligraphy, as well as the special lightning-like Harry Potter font (used for the movie name in Harry Potter movie series).

4) Texture: As the for the texture, my submitted version doesn’t have anything special. But ideally, I hope to add a layer of dot to make it look older just like antique newspapers. Or, a texture of parchment will be helpful as well.
